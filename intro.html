What is a Mindscape?

Mindscape is the first mind-mapping tool designed to visualise intuitive thinking.

Mindscapes are the snapshots of our inner world.

When you are standing on top of a mountain, the landscape is expansively spread out in front of you: hills, valleys, rivers, and lakes...

In a similar way, a "mindscape" provides a map so that we can "see" our thinking. It is as if we were standing outside our own thinking and looking at it objectively.

Self-inquiry

The process of creating a mindcape is called self-inquery, a mindfulness practice that directs attention to the inner awareness of "I". Mindscape as a visual tool, provides an alternative to what it would be like to create a map of thoughts, allowing individuals to explore the thought patterns and relationships from one another. This practice can be helpful for anyone interested in learning more about themselves.

How to Create a Mindscape?

Mindscape is based on Water Logic (1994, Edward de Bono), which metaphorically describes the brain’s thought process as the flow of water, from one thought flows to the next, and eventually forms a cyclical pattern. By mimicking the way our brain is functioning, one’s seemingly chaotic thinking monologue can be reduced to schematics of perceptions that illustrate which are the dominant thoughts and which lead to cyclical patterns.

Step 2: Flow of attention

Flow is the motion of thinking. Like water flows continuously downhill due to gravity, our thinking is driven by the focus of attention...although different from water, in a mindscape, we can easily go back to see where our thoughts had flown through “physically”.

In this step, we learn to connect on thought to another, without repeating itself, until all the thoughts are interconnected. This little excercise is a way when you step out of your thoughts and look at them objetively, can boost up your associative thinking skills.

step 3: map of perceptions

Lastly, Mindscape reduces your complex thoughts into a diagram which helps you identify the relationships between thoughts, the patterns in perceptions.

The most interconnected thoughts are often the important ones one can reflect on. The thought that leads to a circle, often indicates where a cyclical pattern start appearing, which can leads to action of intervention (in the cyclical pattern).
